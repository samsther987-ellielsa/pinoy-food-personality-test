About Adobo

Often called the unofficial national dish of the Philippines, Adobo is a slow-braised dish of meat (usually chicken or pork) cooked in vinegar, soy sauce, garlic, bay leaves, and black peppercorns. Its origins predate Spanish colonization — early Filipinos preserved meat in vinegar, a technique the Spanish called "adobo" (from the Spanish word "adobar," meaning to marinate). Every Filipino family has their own Adobo recipe, passed down through generations, making it one of the most personal dishes in the culture.

Understanding the ISTJ Personality

The ISTJ personality type — known as The Logistician or The Inspector — combines Introversion, Sensing, Thinking, and Judging. ISTJs are responsible, dependable individuals who value tradition, order, and loyalty above all else. They are practical, thorough, and meticulous in everything they do, approaching tasks with a systematic methodology that ensures nothing falls through the cracks. ISTJs form the backbone of many organizations and families, quietly ensuring that everything runs smoothly. Their sense of duty and commitment to their word makes them some of the most reliable people you'll ever meet.
