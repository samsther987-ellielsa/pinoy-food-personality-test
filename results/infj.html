About Kare-Kare

Kare-Kare is a rich peanut-based stew made with oxtail, tripe, and vegetables like banana blossom, eggplant, and string beans. The dish requires patience and skill — the peanut sauce must be thick and velvety, the meat tender enough to fall off the bone. Served with fermented shrimp paste (bagoong) on the side, it's a dish of contrasts and layers. Its origins are debated: some trace it to Indian curry influences, while others connect it to the Kapampangan culinary tradition of Pampanga province.

Understanding the INFJ Personality

The INFJ personality type — known as The Advocate or The Counselor — combines Introversion, Intuition, Feeling, and Judging. INFJs are the rarest personality type, making up about 1-2% of the population. They are deep, insightful individuals who are driven by a strong desire to help others and make the world a better place. INFJs possess an almost uncanny ability to understand people's emotions, motivations, and potential. Their combination of empathy and vision allows them to see what people could become and inspire them toward that growth. Despite their quiet nature, INFJs have a profound impact on those around them.

Strengths & Growth Areas

Core Strengths: Rare depth of insight into human nature, unwavering moral clarity, creative vision that sees beyond what exists to what could be, and genuine empathy that makes people feel profoundly understood. INFJs see patterns in people and events that others miss entirely.

Growth Areas: Perfectionism that can lead to paralysis, tendency toward over-privacy and self-isolation, prone to burnout from absorbing others' pain, difficulty with direct confrontation, and the burden of holding a vision so clear that the present reality perpetually feels inadequate.

Frequently Asked Questions

What does INFJ stand for?

INFJ stands for Introverted, Intuitive, Feeling, and Judging. Known as "The Advocate" or "The Counselor," INFJs are the rarest personality type — making up less than 2% of the population. They combine a deep inner world with a powerful drive to improve the world outside. Despite their introversion, they have genuine warmth for individuals, even if crowds exhaust them.

Why is Kare-Kare the perfect food for INFJ?

Kare-Kare is among the most complex dishes in Filipino cuisine: layers of peanut sauce, oxtail, vegetables, and bagoong working in precise harmony, requiring hours of careful preparation. It's not a dish made for instant gratification — it rewards patience, attention, and depth. Like the INFJ, Kare-Kare is layered, nuanced, and incomparably rich once you take the time to truly understand it.

What are the best careers for INFJ?

INFJs find purpose in work that channels their vision and empathy toward meaningful change. Top career paths include: psychology and counseling, writing and literature, healthcare (especially holistic medicine), nonprofit leadership, social justice advocacy, human rights law, spiritual guidance, educational curriculum development, and qualitative research. They need work that feels purposeful — otherwise they feel deeply unfulfilled.

What is the INFJ "door slam"?

The "INFJ door slam" refers to the type's tendency to abruptly and completely cut off relationships where their values have been repeatedly violated or they've felt consistently unheard. Unlike most types who gradually distance, INFJs often appear patient right up until they're permanently done. It happens rarely, but when it does, there's typically no going back. Understanding this about yourself as an INFJ — or about the INFJ in your life — can help prevent avoidable ruptures through earlier, honest communication.
